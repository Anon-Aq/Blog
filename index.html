How to get rid of fungus gnats

What are Fungus Gnats?

Fungus gnats are tiny, little flying insects, black and stripy gray in color, stripy around the stomach, similar to mosquitoes in appearance, distantly related to them, often mistaken for mosquitoes. Fungus gnats have long legs compared to their bodies, and light transparent, see through wings, which are not very strong. Fungus gnats are weak fliers, but contrary to popular belief, they are able to reach heights. Fungus gnats are usually no more than 2-5mm, sometimes longer. They are a gardener, plant enthusiast, and less often, people living in damp housing's worst nightmare. They are extremely annoying because they breed very fast. You will usually first notice them when they fly towards your face, especially around the eyes and mouth, where moisture, which they are attracted to, and CO2, which they are also attracted to is found. Fungus gnats are most noticeable during daylight hours or night hours, where lighting is found, and in damp, wet weather and warm seasons. Fungus gnats, despite being related to mosquitos, do not bite, but they can do a lot of damage to the roots of your plants.
